Why buyers use Alloc8r

Without help, chasing a hard-to-find car means months of vague timelines and empty promises. Alloc8r sources the dealer who can deliver first and negotiates the best deal. You arrive as a high-volume, low-margin client, your allocation already waiting, and your time never wasted on a waitlist.

The Problem

The Waitlist Is a Trap

The moment you hand over a deposit, it becomes an interest-free loan for the dealer, with no guaranteed delivery timeline. The Lexus GX 550, Porsche 911, and G 63 currently average 2 to 4 years on a standard waitlist, and most buyers are given an optimistic estimate just to lock in their money.

The Alloc8r Fix

We Hold Allocations. You Skip the Queue.

We pre-secure allocations through our dealer network so we can transfer them directly to you. Leon was told 3 years by his dealer. He had his 2026 Lexus GX 550 in 2 weeks through Alloc8r.

The Problem

The Price You See Isn't the Price You Pay

Nitrogen tires, paint protection, admin fees, extended warranties. None of it is mandatory, all of it is profitable. Most buyers don't find out until they're already in the finance office, pen in hand.

The Alloc8r Fix

We Strip the Extras. You Keep the Savings.

We go line by line through every deal and remove anything you didn't ask for. Sally's Toyota Sienna quote had $5,500 in hidden markup baked in. We brought it down to $1,500, keeping only the add-ons she actually wanted.

The Problem

The back-and-forth, the manager disappearing, the finance office at the end. It's not accidental. You either pay extra to leave, or spend 4–5 hours in the finance office convinced you finally got a good deal. You didn't.

You Show Up Once. To Sign.

Trade-ins are one of the most common places dealers quietly take money from buyers, and one of the areas where we add the most value.

We don't just accept the purchasing dealer's first offer. We shop your trade-in across our private network of wholesalers to find the highest market value. We also factor in the provincial tax credit you receive when trading in, in BC, for example, you only pay PST on the difference between the new car price and your trade-in value, which can be worth thousands depending on the deal structure.

In some cases, selling privately nets you more even after accounting for the lost tax credit. We run the numbers both ways and tell you which path puts more money in your pocket.
